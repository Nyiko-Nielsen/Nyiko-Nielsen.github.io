1. Classify the data subject

  • If you engage in direct marketing (via electronic means):
  • Determine in which category a data subject falls, i.e.:
  • have they given their consent for the processing of their information (for the purpose of direct marketing); or
  • are they already a customer of your company.


2. Obtain consent in the prescribed manner and form

  • Approach a data subject who has not yet given their consent if:
  • their consent is required (i.e. they are not a customer); and
  • they have not previously withheld their consent.
  • Keep a record of the data subjects that you have approached, as you may only approach a data subject once.


3. Comply with specific processing requirements

  • Ensure that the customer information has been obtained:
  • in the context of the sale of a product or service; and
  • for the purpose of direct marketing of your own products or services.
  • Give the data subject a reasonable opportunity to freely and informally object to the use of their electronic details at the time of obtaining their details and every time communication is sent to them.
  • Check that all direct marketing communications contain:
  • details of the identity of the sender; and
  • an address or contact details to which the data subject can object to receiving such information in future.
